Cara Miller: Finding Community Through Art and Advocacy

Type: AP Format Profile | Published: Student Zine | Fall 2024

A profile of Cara Miller, a local artist and LGBTQ+ advocate whose work bridges creative expression and community organizing. Through interviews and observation, the piece explores how Miller uses art as a tool for building belonging and visibility in queer spaces, particularly for transgender and nonbinary youth navigating identity and acceptance. Written in Associated Press style, demonstrating professional journalism format and interview-based reporting.

Building Community Through Liberation: App State Student Connects Movements On and Off Campus

Type: AP Format Profile | February 23, 2026

A profile of Katie, a senior double majoring in philosophy and Gender, Women's and Sexuality Studies at Appalachian State University. As president of Triota and the Philosophy Club and a core organizer with Tempest, a socialist organizing group, Katie connects campus and community movements through coalition-building and a philosophy rooted in mutual aid. The piece traces their path from rural Pennsylvania to App State, including academic probation, an animal ethics course that changed everything, and winning the Tom Regan Animal Rights Research Fellowship. Written in Associated Press style.

Art & Legacy

Type: Documentary Script | Length: 2-3 minutes | Fall 2025

A documentary script profiling two High Country artists—Laura Temple, a painter, and Donald Quinn, a luthier—exploring how their creative work honors family legacy and creates lasting connections across generations. The script weaves together interview footage with visual storytelling to examine themes of memory, craft, and the intimate relationship between artists and their materials. Demonstrates two-column AV script format with detailed shot descriptions and audio cues.
