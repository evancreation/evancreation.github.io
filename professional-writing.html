Representing the Unheard: Power, Visibility, and Voice in Social Justice Movements

Award: Outstanding Gender, Women's & Sexuality Studies Essay | Fall 2024 | Type: Research Paper

An exploration of how social justice movements navigate questions of representation, visibility, and voice. This paper examines the tension between the need for marginalized communities to be heard and the risks of tokenization, co-optation, and representational harm. Drawing on feminist theory and case studies from contemporary activism, the essay argues for approaches to visibility that center community autonomy and challenge rather than reinforce existing power structures.

Cara Miller: Finding Community Through Art and Advocacy

Type: Profile | Published: Student Zine | Fall 2024

A profile of Cara Miller, a local artist and LGBTQ+ advocate whose work bridges creative expression and community organizing. Through interviews and observation, the piece explores how Miller uses art as a tool for building belonging and visibility in queer spaces, particularly for transgender and nonbinary youth navigating identity and acceptance.
