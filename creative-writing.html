Cyborgs Aren't Experiments

Status: Shortlisted for publication in Ink Tide anthology, forthcoming 2026 | Genre: Speculative Fiction

A speculative fiction story examining bodily autonomy and medical ethics through the lens of cyborg identity. The narrative follows a character navigating a world where technological augmentation is commonplace but the line between "enhancement" and "experimentation" remains dangerously blurred, particularly for marginalized bodies. The story challenges assumptions about consent, progress, and who gets to define what counts as "improvement."

Your Components Are Showing

Genre: Speculative Fiction | 2025

A short story exploring visibility, passing, and the politics of disclosure in a future where biomedical augmentation is normalized but not universally accepted. Through the experiences of a character whose prosthetics are unexpectedly revealed, the story examines how technology intersects with identity and the pressures to conform to normative ideas of embodiment.
