Unseen Workers of the Union

Type: Mini-Documentary | Duration: 4:23 | Fall 2025

A mini-documentary profiling Paul Ford, evening operations supervisor at Appalachian State University's Plemons Student Union. After 21 years of service, Ford reflects on his work creating the "living room of campus" and mentoring student workers who go on to achieve their goals.

This film explores the often-invisible labor that makes campus life possible, highlighting the dedication of staff who shape student experiences behind the scenes. Through Ford's perspective, the documentary examines themes of labor visibility, mentorship, and the transformative power of responsibility.

My Role: Script writing, audio recording, narration, and editing. Collaborated with director Nashbi Cruz-Martinez and camera operator Micheal Aveni to craft a narrative highlighting the essential but often overlooked contributions of campus staff.

On and On

Type: Short Narrative Film | Duration: 1:30 | Spring 2026

A short film about the insidious pull of screens and algorithms. The way passive consumption quietly becomes something harder to name. Gabe sits in front of static, already gone. What follows is less a haunting than a handshake: the screen doesn't force him anywhere. It just makes it easy to go.

The film uses intercutting, ADR, and an original opening animation to build unease out of stillness. The horror isn't loud. It's familiar.

My Role: Editing, ADR production and integration, opening title animation.

Scramble King

Type: Mini-Documentary Edit | Spring 2026 | Footage: EditStock

Emilio Hernandez is a Brazilian jiu-jitsu competitor from Spring Hill, Tennessee, a place most people don't associate with the sport, which is part of what makes him interesting. He trains under Ray Casias, spars with UFC and Bellator fighters, and carries the nickname "Scramble King" for a style built on mobility and controlled chaos. His goal is a black belt world championship under the IBJJF.

The edit builds his portrait through interview pacing and training footage, letting his voice carry the structure while the cuts reflect his approach to the mat: deliberate, never static.

My Role: Editor. Footage provided by EditStock.

Crossover: Scene Edit

Type: Continuity Editing | Spring 2026 | Footage: EditStock

A scene from Crossover, written by Mohanalakshmi Rajakumar and Kali Bailey. Mala is 18, quietly obsessed with basketball, and navigating a dinner table conversation with her mother Kavitha (who has different plans for her) and her Aunt Anitha, who is very much in on the secret. It's a three-character scene built on subtext: what's said, what's covered up, and what passes between Mala and Anitha in a single glance.

The edit focuses on continuity, screen direction, and using coverage to track the shifting power dynamics across the table.

My Role: Editor. Footage provided by EditStock.

J Pop: :60 Commercial Edit

Type: Commercial Edit | Duration: :60 | Spring 2026 | Footage: EditStock

Ultra-slow-motion 1000fps footage of popsicle sticks exploding under tension, cut into a 60-second commercial with a simple through-line: boy sees girl, sets up the moment, sticks land right. The footage does the spectacle; the edit does the story.

My Role: Editor. Footage provided by EditStock.
